Words of Advice
Tips I heard that actually helped. Feel free to listen — or not. It's up to you.
1
Get fee waivers. Apply for free.
All 30 of the schools I applied to were completely free. Fee waivers are widely available — through CommonApp, QuestBridge, school websites, and more. There's no reason to pay to apply.
2
Be niche. Be yourself.
No one likes a basic, run-of-the-mill applicant. Admissions officers read thousands of essays. The ones that stand out are specific, personal, and genuine — not the ones that sound impressive.
3
Use Early Decision strategically.
If you want to get into a top school and prestige matters to you, ED gives you a real boost. But it's binding — only go ED if you've done your research and you're genuinely committed to that school.
4
Don't limit yourself.
Apply to as many schools as you want. You miss 100% of the shots you don't take. With fee waivers, there's nothing stopping you from casting a wide net.
5
Have fun with it.
Applying to college gets boring and tedious when you aren't obsessed with the process. If you can find something exciting about it — the research, the writing, the strategy — it becomes a lot more bearable.
